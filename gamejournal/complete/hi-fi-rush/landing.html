Hi-Fi RUSH

Release Date: January 25th, 2023
Genre: Action Rhythm
Developer: Tango Gameworks
Platform: Playstation 5, Xbox Series X, PC
Details: Steam

Hi-Fi RUSH is an interesting game. It's a level-based action game, where each level takes the player through a different area of the company complex the story takes place within. As you progress through a level, it segments itself between platforming and combat styles of gameplay. During the platforming sections of the level, your free to explore your surroundings, look for potential secrets, and might have to do some light puzzle solving. While you explore, you'll come across arena-looking areas where you'll transition to the combat portion of the level. Here is where you'll use light and heavy attacks to fight against a variety of robots that want to stop you. The core difference that this game has to similar ones is many of the actions in which occur happen to the beat of the music. Your attacks do more damage while on beat, enemies will perform their attacks to the beat, and your surroundings move to the beat while platforming.

The story and characters within the game is enjoyable. While the story at it's core is simple, it's a breath of fresh air from the more densely packed RPG stories I've been playing lately. The characters have well fleshed-out personalities and has a nice sense of humor all throughout the time I spent with the game. While I might have not of been glued to my chair wondering what was going to happen next, it was pleasant listening to each of them talking with each other with whatever silly quips they had to say. Visually the game is great, the cell-shading style gives it this near comic book feel.

However, this was a game I had to put down and I don't think I'll come back to. That's not to say this is a bad game, but I don't think it's a game for me. I'm not the most rhythmically inclined, where the only rhythm-based game I've really enjoyed is Beat Saber. Most of the time I couldn't stay on beat, even with the visual aid. Eventually I reached the point where you unlock a Parry skill, where you can counterattack enemies with a more strictly timed action. While that's cool, there's enemies that nearly require you to parry against them to finish them off or to even defeat them. With my lack of rhythm, that became more frustrating than a challenge. This is definitely a "me" problem than the game's, as for the early stages of the game were really fun! So, if you're intro rhythm-based gameplay and want an action game that incorporates that into its core, then this game is 100% for you.
