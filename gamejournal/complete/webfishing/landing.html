Webfishing

Release Date: October 11th, 2024
Genre: Fishing Chatroom
Developer: lamedeveloper
Platform: PC
Details: Steam

Webfishing is a small game made by lamedeveloper that allows you to make small lobbies to go fishing with your friends. I heard about this game through Jello Plays Games and it looked like a fun little game to play with friends! While there's not too much to do, it's the perfect game for just having something to do while in a voice chat with the friends.

The game takes place in a small forested area along the coast. Given your starter rod, you wander around and fish for the various species. The map is scattered with various different interactions to give the world a bit of life, like benches to fish from, mushrooms to launch you across the map, and paved areas that you draw on with some chalk. When a fish bites your line, a small mini-game starts where you have to hold and mash your mouse button to fill the bar. There's quite a few types of fish, with different groups between fresh and saltwater. Each species has different rarities as well, progressively growing in their worth based on their rarity. While you're wandering around and fishing, rainstorms can cover parts of the map, or a portal to the void will appear!

While As you generate money from your fish, you can buy various upgrades, tools, and cosmetics from the shops! A lot of the extra tools add some small fun activities to do outside of fishing, some chalk to draw on the pavement, a metal detector to hunt for hidden treasure, and boxing gloves to punch your friends into the water! The variety of cosmetics is quite large, between the various facial features and clothing options. There's also your camp rank that you can upgrade to unlock further options, like particle affects!

And... that's really it. It's a really simple game, but it's perfect for when I don't want to play anything highly demanding while talking with people. Not many of my friends own it, so it's hard to get a full experience of the game and joining public lobbies just isn't my thing. It's not a game that I'll come home to play, but just to throw on while doing other things. It's fun for that, and interacting with the friend who does own the game has been pretty fun! It's a cute, simple game, and would recommend it if you'll looking for just a chill game to play in the background.
