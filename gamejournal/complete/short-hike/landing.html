A Short Hike

Release Date: April 5th, 2019
Genre: Casual Adventure
Developer: Adam Robinson-Yu
Platform: Nintendo Switch, Playstation 4, Xbox One, and PC
Details: Website

A Short Hike is a short little adventure game that puts you in the shoes of Claire, a young bird from the city who've visiting Hawk Peak Provincial Park with their aunt. She's waiting for a phone call, but her aunt tells her there's no cell reception except for maybe at the top of Hawk Peak. Claire then sets off into the park to find her way to the top of the peak for some reception. During her trek, she meets other visitors to the park, even helps them out with some of their own problems. It's a cozy adventure where there's no real danger or threat, just exploring the woods and following the mountain trail.

I enjoyed my time with this game! It has a nice retro charm, as you can tell it's a fully 3-D game, but has this pixelated filter overtop that's reminiscent of an older style. The gameplay is quite easy to pickup, as Claire's movement options are very similar to other adventure games: expending stamina to run, jump, and climb. As you gather golden feathers to expand your stamina, the park becomes less daunting and more of a playground for you to run around within. During these adventures, the other visitor's you can interact with has this silly charm to them, as they'll talk with Claire in short-form text messages. It just overall put a big smile on my face the whole way through, and was a cozy adventure to embark on for an evening.
