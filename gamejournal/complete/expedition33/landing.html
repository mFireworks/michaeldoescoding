Clair Obscur: Expedition 33

Release Date: April 24th, 2025
Genre: Turn-Based RPG
Developer: Sandfall Interactive
Platform: Playstation 5, Xbox Series X/S, and PC
Details: Website

This is the first game developed by Sandfall Interactive, and man they hit it out of the park. The game feels like a classical turn-based RPG, with an engaging story that kept pulling me forward in anticipation for what happens next. I didn't know much about the game when it first launched, mainly just heard about it from podcasts that I follow and how much they were enjoying the game, saying it was easily an early contender for Game of the Year. That made me put it on the backlog, and finally picked it up during the big summer sale. I'm really glad I went into the game without knowing anything, as the prologue really hooks you into the world, clearly showing what's at stake for these expeditioners. The game had me hooked, and enjoyed most of my time with the game!

Gameplay

As mentioned above, this game feels very much like traditional turn-based RPGs, where you're following a main questline through various areas, defeating the enemies within to reach your objective. Entering combat, you have basic attacks and items, but you can also spend your Action Points to cast various different skills your characters have equipped. When the enemy attacks, you can time dodges to avoid damage, or use the tighter parry to perform a counterattack if you parry all their attacks! It reminded me a lot of the Mario & Luigi games in a way, as they follow that same formula. I never got tired of the fights, and as you progress they don't overstay their welcome, in with the right setups you can disperse whole fights before they get a chance to attack.

What made the fights so interesting though was how they implemented the skills you could learn. Instead with each character with their own skill tree, you find various "pictos" around the world that augment your character in some way. After a couple of fights, you gain the ability to learn the picto without having it equipped by using your skill points to enable the picto. As you leveled up or found skill points in the world, the amount of skills you can have equipped at a single time increased, opening the path towards building your characters to fit your playstyle. I really liked this, as it forced me to try out all the new skills I found while exploring, do a bit of grinding to unlock them fully, and have me learn synergies between it and the ones I already had equipped. It was freeing from the rigid skill trees I've been interacting with lately, and had the characters feel catered toward how I liked to play.

Exploration was also enjoyable throughout the game. Most of the areas are fairly linear in their design, with some branching paths for optional pictos and currency, but not so much that it wasn't clear as to where to go. There are plenty of optional areas on the world map to explore, which were nice to explore to maintain my level without needing to dedicate time towards grinding. Fights remained hard despite all my sidetracking, so might be tough to just mainline the main story. While it didn't feel like much sidetracking in the moment, looking back on it, I'd spend whole play sessions just doing side content, so while there was a lot, I was having a blast with them!

There's other systems at play here, like different weapons to choose and level and a rigid skill tree for unlocking in-combat skills, but there's not much to touch on there as they follow pretty standard RPG systems. I do like how the only equipment to worry about was your weapon, enabled skills, and chosen combat abilities, as I didn't find myself constantly having to tinker with my equipment, sell outdated gear, or juggle my skills. Your weapons act more like play styles, and grow in strength alongside you. It felt very streamlined, and focused more on the combat, exploration, and story, which is the meat and potatoes for me!

Story & Worldbuilding

I'm not going to get into details of the story, as I believe going into this game without much knowledge of its story really amplified my attachment to it. The really basic hook is, your "party" is apart of a larger expedition that is leaving the safety of the city for the mainland, in search of answers as to why a calamity happens each year. The game is split into three distinct acts, each unveiling a overarching goal for the act, while also introducing more questions into the state of the world. I feel it's very well written, the characters feel human, as they're just doing what they can in this, what feels like, impossible task. I did find the last act to be a bit confusing, as it was a huge lore dump into a bunch of stuff that I was unaware of at the time. Though as the act progressed, little nuggets from previous acts began to make a lot more sense, tying it closer together, but still was a lot to dump all at once.

As you're exploring the continent, it really puts forward that you're exploring a fantasy world, with varying different unnaturally colored forests, to snow-covered abandoned train stations, to walking on the bottom of a sea floor, watching fish swim above you. I found myself stopping every so often to just take in the scenery, as they were just wonderful environments to explore. They also build the world in that, you'll find various grapple points and handholds in these areas to help aid your travels. In other games, these tend to just be outcroppings of rocks, or specific material you can grapple to. Here, these points have been placed by previous expeditions, explaining their existence and building into this idea of the expeditions being this multi-generational endeavor, with each one carving the path for the next.

There's a lot more going on here throughout the story that you'll have to uncover yourself, it's a fantastic story to experience and I cannot praise it enough.

Audio & Visuals

I've mentioned how great the environments look, but this also applies into combat as well! The foes you'll face have tons of detail to them, and tie in well into the areas in which you fight them. You'll find beasts covered in coral in the underwater area, or creatures made out of sap within the forests. While you're performing your attacks, the camera also pans with the attacks, making them feel dynamic and emphasize their weight upon impact. The music also adds a ton to the overall "vibe", with serene melodies to match the flow of the environment, or the heavy choir to help raise the tension of a fight. There's also some that are just absolute jams that I just had to stop and jam to the sweet jazz!

Overall Thoughts

This was probably my favorite game of the year so far, as the game excelled into both its story and gameplay. The story hooked me from the get-go and unveiled just enough to answer questions of mine, but also present new ones to keep me hooked. It confused me quite a bit near the end, but that's more of the "carpet pulled from under me" than a hindrance to the story. Without that, I don't think that the ending would've hit the same as it did. The gameplay was fantastic, as each area was a joy to explore, as they provided enough resources and experience to keep my party's power up with the difficulty without the need to grind. There were areas that put my skill to the test, so it wasn't a walk in the park either. If you enjoy the classic RPG experience, then this is an incredible game to play and cannot recommend it enough!
