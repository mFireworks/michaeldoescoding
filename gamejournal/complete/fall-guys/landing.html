Fall Guys

Release Date: August 4th, 2020
Genre: Platformer Battle Royale
Developer: Mediatonic
Platform: Nintendo Switch, Playstation 5, Xbox Series X, and PC
Details: Website

Fall Guys is a Platformer Battle Royale game developed by Mediatonic. This game gained a ton of popularity back when it released in 2020, but kind of fell into obscurity over the years. I would see the game in my library from time to time, and remember the fond memories I had of it back then. Recently, my friend group was looking for something we could all play together, and I talked them into trying the game with me. I wanted to return to it anyways to see if was as fun as I remembered it, which I'm happy to report that it is, with some caveats.

In essence, this is a purely multiplayer game that reminds me a lot of the old Wipeout TV show, of players navigating various obstacle courses, with each round eliminating a portion of players until one is remaining. These may require the player to reach the end of the course as fast as they can or survive an arena the longest. Majority of these challenges require platforming skills to progress through the course, avoid obstacles, or reach specific areas quickly. Unlike the TV show though, all players are competing at the same time, so you can effect how other players progress themselves by moving platforms away from them, grabbing them, or just simply get in their way. It's a chaotic mess, but it's a fun mess.

That's all their really is for the gameplay of the game. There is a wide variety of courses to play, ranging from a standard obstacle course, to running away from slime on a circular course, to testing your memory of how many fruit are present. Along with courses made by the developers, they've introduced a level editor for players to make their own courses, which get added to the pool of available courses. I think that's a neat way to keep a course rotation fairly fresh, by allowing the players to make their own experiences. However, player made courses aren't always... quality, so if you don't want to play on those, there are other modes that are solely official courses, which also include team-based courses to spice things up!

As you compete, you'll earn points for the Fame Pass, which is just a battle pass like in other live service games. While there are items locked behind a paywall (mainly the skins), there is quite a few things available for free. While the pass rotates multiple times within a session, there's also a Crown Pass, which you progress through as you finish in first, and is completely free. While it's harder to get wins, it's nice that wins are rewarded for all players. The passes can give you currency to spend in the shop for various cosmetics, with lots of tie-ins from other companies and series.

Overall Thoughts

Yeah, that's the entirety of the game. Participate in various courses to progress through the battle pass, to unlock more currency to spend in the shop to show off in the game. It's a tried and true live-service model, but it gets stale rather quickly, especially when playing for free. With the battle pass being the main method of progression, the only meaningful way to progress through it is to complete the daily challenges. These challenges give an astonishing amount of points, nearly equivalent to participating in 5 games up to the final round. So each day you'll make a lot of progress initially, but then slow to a crawl once those challenges are completed. While having user-created maps available, the rotation of courses is still repetitive, only utilizing the highly-rated maps. Which makes sense, don't want the playerbase to have to be exposed to low-level game design, but they might be a bit too strict on maps they allow. The pricing for cosmetics seems a bit aggressive and all over the place. Some items you have to purchase with the in-game currency, but others have a flat cost on them. Paid skins rotate in daily, sticking around for a couple of days, but I have yet to see the free skins rotate out once. It feels like Fortnite in terms of a live-service, but as a silly platformer.

Despite these gripes, it's still pretty damn fun with friends. Since it's a free-to-play, crossplay game, nearly my whole gaming group picked it up and started playing with each other. We've been having a blast with it, us either working together or trolling each other. All of us watching the last of the group barely finishing, or cheering on the last remaining player of our squad. It's a nice change of pace, especially when we've been struggling to find a game we could all play. I tried playing a bit solo, but it just wasn't as fun and put it down around one round. Not sure how much I'd recommend it since it gets stale rather quickly, but it's pretty fun to pick up and play with friends every so often!
