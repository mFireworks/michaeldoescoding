Minecraft Dungeons

Release Date: May 26th, 2020
Genre: Dungeon Crawling Looter
Developer: Mojang Studios, Double Eleven
Platform: Nintendo Switch, Playstation 4, Xbox One, and PC
Details: Website

Minecraft Dungeons is a dungeon crawling looter game developed by Mojang Studios. This is Minecraft's third spin-off game, that has you traveling across the overworld slaying hoards of monsters that are being commanded by the Arch-Illager. As you progress through the world, you'll collect many weapons, armor, and artifacts to power up your characters, allowing you to travel to further lands or take on harder difficulties for better loot.

I did a solo play-through of this back when it originally came out, then never really came back to it. My good friend Benito was telling me about his recent play-through with his wife, and they were have a blast with it as a couch co-op game. Finding good couch co-op games seems to be rather hard for my fiancee and I, so I convinced Valerie to give it a try with me and it's been fun! I was a little worried that the gameplay would be too different for her to have fun, but she's picked it up pretty quickly and was quite happy to easily kill skeletons that have given her so much trouble in Minecraft itself.

Gameplay

The game's core loop is you travel to various different locations fitting within a Minecraft world, and fight your way through hoards of monsters and illagers to the end of the level. As you progress, defeated monsters may drop new equipment to upgrade your character with. These could be new weapons of various styles, armor with different passives, or artifacts that grant you new abilities. On top of the randomized type of loot, weapons and armor also get random enchantment choices for your to pick from, allowing you to build specific playstyles that synergize these enchantments and abilities together. You gain levels, but your main progression is tied to the "level" of your gear, which higher level gear drops from higher difficulty runs.

The goal is to continue finding better and better gear to aid you in taking on higher difficulties for the same levels. We've only made it pass the final boss of the campaign, but the game opens up beyond that with even higher difficulties, high-end currency to fully customize your equipment, and new levels with specific twists to them. It's a rather simple gameplay loop, but it's enjoyable for us since it plays really well as a couch co-op game. We're building our characters around each other's playstyles, and exchanging gear that we know the other would prefer to use. Valerie is not as hardcore of a gamer as I, but the fact that we're both able to feel challenged and have fun laying together means the game is doing something right!

To add on to the post game, the game also features "Daily Trails", which contain modifiers on existing levels to benefit or hinder certain playstyles. They offer better rewards for completion, while also forcing you to try out other styles of play. There's a "Tower" challenge that acts like a rouge-like experience of building a character from scratch, selecting randomized gear on the completion of each floor. We also beat the final boss of the tower, but was rewarded handsomely despite our defeat. It's the same core gameplay, but varies slightly to provide unique experiences. The only thing that I don't like is the inclusion of paid "adventure passes" within the game. The game already has 6 DLCs and 3 season passes, it just feels a little scummy locking more content behind a season pass when the game isn't modeled as a "live service" game. It's only cosmetics from what I can tell, but just doesn't sit right with me.

Story & Worldbuilding

There is a bit of a story the game, but doesn't feel all the "grand" as they make it out to be. It's presented like the end of days is coming due to the Arch-Illager's power, but the narrator adds little quips that pull you out of it, or when the Arch-Illager ambushes you, he teases you at the same level of a grade school bully. I know the game is meant to be appropriate for children, just made the story feel silly enough to just not care. There's never a sense of urgency, even when the game kind of encourages you to revisit areas to grind for specific gear on higher difficulties. The story just didn't add a lot, and moreso felt like a gate to enter the true scope of the game.

I will say though, some of the locations within the game are inspiring. It's a Minecraft game, so seeing some of these locations helped spark some inspiration for my own Minecraft world. The one that comes to mind is the Redstone Mines, it was just a well presented mining operation that had this dwarven architecture. I always struggled with underground builds, but this sparked some ideas that I could apply to help bring those to the next level of design.

Overall Thoughts

Not much to say on visual or audio, it looked like a Minecraft game for sure. It's a simple game that I think hits the mark as a good couch co-op game. It encourages teamwork in defeating monsters and sharing equipment with each other, but allows to scale the difficulty around how you want to play. There's not a big time investment for the game, with most levels ranging between 10-20 minutes to clear, allowing it to fit nicely in our busy days. I'm glad Valerie has been enjoying it as much as I, as finding a co-op game that we both enjoy and want to stick with has been difficult. The game feels like a frozen pizza, it's not the best of its kind, but it's simple, good, and doesn't take long to start enjoying your time with it.
